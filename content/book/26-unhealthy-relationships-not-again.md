# Not Again

Two whole months of actual, honest-to-goodness bliss.

And then?

I still don't actually know.

That December, Tinder Guy went overseas for a month to visit his brother.

I looked after his house and saw to his animals. I felt great that he trusted me with all this.

I went far above and beyond the call of duty. I washed all his linen. Scrubbed his shower till it sparkled. Bleached the toilet.

Clipped the lawn with nail scissors.

I wasn't just house-sitting. I was campaigning.

Campaigning for him to be impressed with me.

I played maid, security guard, horticulturist, dog-walker, dog-feeder, and dog-cuddler over the festive season (when I should have been spending time with my own family).

While he was away, communication dwindled. If I received one text a day, it was a lot. I tried to contact him with house-related questions, and the calls were cut. When we made plans to Facetime, he flaked.

I told myself that the six-hour time difference was to blame, and tried really hard not to be needy. First prize was that he was having a great time, and so not hearing from him was a good sign, right?

Besides, I had my maid duties to attend to.

On the day he was scheduled to return home – the day I'd taken leave from work, the day I'd bought the ingredients to make his favourite meal, the day that I'd organised a babysitter, the day that I'd woken up so excited for his return – he simply didn't arrive.

I couldn't get hold of him and was beside myself with worry that something terrible had happened to him. So, I sat in his house for hours, waiting in anguish for a message that everything was okay.

In a normal relationship, I might have called his friends or family to ask whether they had heard from him, but it suddenly dawned on me that I didn't have any of their numbers.

Also, none of them knew I existed.

I'd never met his friends.

I'd never met any of his family members.

I'd never met any of his colleagues.

I had no clue how to get hold of him, so all I could do was sit and wait.

And check my phone 370 million times.

The following day, he responded to my frantic messages, telling me that I had messed up the dates.

Really?

He said that he was only scheduled to arrive the following day, and that he couldn't believe that I was such a klutz.

Was I?

He did indeed return the next day. He didn't comment on his pristine house. He didn't invite me to stay for the dinner I had cooked to celebrate his return. He didn't thank me for keeping his dogs alive, or for making sure criminals didn't go shopping in his garage. He seemed resentful that I was in his house at all, or that I'd had the temerity to water his plants for him.

(Okay, fine, I did forget to turn the sprinkler off one night, and ran up a pretty breathtaking water bill, right after I drowned a few of the less resilient plants. But he didn't know about that yet.)

Weeks went by like that: cold shoulders, clipped responses, calculated distance. He seemed resentful that I was in his life. There were so many things that were amiss, that reeked of his prior behaviour.

Two wine glasses in the sink when I arrived at the house. Lipstick on the rim. Pairs of coffee cups in the bedroom. A different shampoo in the shower – floral, not his style.

Definitely not mine.

Sure, he was allowed to have friends over. Friends who left lipstick-stained wine glasses. Friends who drank coffee in his bedroom and used shampoo that smelled like frangipani and deceit.

No?

I didn't ask him about this outright, because I was afraid of appearing nosey or high maintenance.

'Oh, did you get new shampoo?' I'd enquire breezily. 'I quite like the smell of this one.'

'Yeah, it does smell good, hey,' he'd say, without meeting my gaze.

The more things didn't add up, the more I grew sceptical.

Suspicious.

Okay, fine, obsessed.

It's the stuff that all harmonious relationships are made of.

I knew – deep down – that there was someone else. But my heart refused to believe he'd do it again. Not after everything. Not now that we were exclusive.

I couldn't fathom that someone whom I believed to be caring, understanding, and empathetic could have made me wait for so long and pull me through the wringer, only to obliterate my heart.

Again.

He became belligerent. He accused me of overthinking everything. He got mad because he felt I was 'digging' into his personal life. It wasn't like I had to dig very far; I simply pointed out things which were glaringly obvious. Like ladies' boxer shorts that weren't mine on the washing line, or girls' facial products that weren't mine in the bathroom, or a bra that wasn't mine in his bedroom.

I'm no quack, and I wouldn't want to misdiagnose anything or label anyone out of turn, but if it looks like a duck and sounds like a duck...

The duck is probably a narcissist.

After a while, he didn't even try to hide the shit anymore. He didn't dash to the bathroom to conceal the extra (pink) toothbrush. He didn't carefully slip someone else's hair-ties into his pocket when he thought I wasn't looking. He simply left it all out for me to see.

Bloody hell, if you're going to be a cheater, at least do it well!

It felt like he was insulting my intelligence.

But I decided that I wasn't going to stoop to such levels of craziness that the bats would fly out of my belfry.

I mean, I could understand why other people might start stalking the bastard during the day rather than going to work.

Of course, I wouldn't do this.

Because, whacko!

Jokes.

I did it.

I stalked him.

I skipped work to stalk him.

I went as far as borrowing a colleague's vehicle so he wouldn't recognise my car as I drove past when he was at home.

Clever.

You would think he would be at work during work hours, right?

Wrong.

He was a sales rep. He had a flexible schedule and could see prospective clients at his convenience. His work hours were entirely his own.

His personal hours were apparently someone else's.

And that someone else was not me.

Frequently, there was another car in his driveway. Yet I didn't really know what to do with that knowledge, other than to sit and stew about it. It wasn't like I had the confidence to ring the doorbell and invite myself inside.

What was I going to say? 'Hello, I'm here to discuss your product but, evidently, you are double-booked. I'll come back when another person's car is not in your fucking driveway. And when your product isn't in her...'

Why did I persist in this practice that caused me so much pain? It hurt like hell, but I kept on driving down the same road (literally and metaphorically).

I needed to know the truth. To prove to myself that I wasn't fabricating all the suspicions through my own insecurity.

I was secure.

Totally stable.

Definitely not crazy.

And dignified.

Definitely dignified.

I could understand how someone else in this situation might become so frantic that when they were in the throes of an argument, they would screech like a tormented barn owl. And then fall to the carpet in fits of sobs.

Not me, though.

I couldn't afford carpet.

I had cheap, wood-look laminate flooring that was lifting in places from moisture damage.

Possibly from all my tears.

'Is this how it's going to be?' he'd ask with disdain when I began flapping like that barn owl.

'Is it really necessary to have an all-fall-down over something you've persuaded yourself of?' he leered.

'You've already made up your mind, so what's the point in even trying to defend myself?' he sneered.

'Are you seriously going to choose today to cry and perform? When I've got so much going on?' he'd exclaim in disbelief.

'Why would I want to call you, when every time we talk, you bring up our relationship and are determined to fight with me about it?' he would note with reproach.

With all the gaslighting going on, I'm surprised there wasn't a fiery explosion. Maybe it was better that I didn't have real wooden floors.

The worst part is that all of his accusations somehow made a weird sort of sense.

Why would he want to be with someone who was acting crazy, who was stalking him, who was obsessed and teary?

Many years later, having now ruminated on it and examined the situation from every conceivable direction, I have a lot to say about this.

This isn't a confession. It's a cautionary tale in the making.

*polite cough while ascending soapbox*

Firstly, I feel I owe every self-respecting woman out there an explanation.

Beginning by noting that I was not a self-respecting woman at the time.

This is probably where I should descend from the soapbox to preserve my own dignity, but I'm not done yet, Goddamnit.

Ladies, don't fall for this trap. I was so desperate for connection that I no longer cared what the connection was.

Love? Hate? Didn't matter – as long as something still tied us together, I clung to it.

Because letting go of the feeling would mean letting go of him.

And I wasn't ready to do that.

I loved him so much I felt I couldn't live without him – and yet, I hated him with a ferocity I hadn't known I possessed.

Both emotions at the same time.

Not confusing at all.

Hate like that doesn't appear out of nowhere. It smoulders from the charred remains of a love that once consumed everything.

In unhealthy relationships, hate and love are often two sides of the same coin.

An alliance as unholy as the one Tinder Guy was nurturing with the owner of the pink toothbrush.

(My toothbrush was yellow, for the record. Always has been. Positive burst of sunshine that I am.)

*less polite cough*

So, I stayed.

Not out of love.

Not even out of hope.

I remained tethered – not by longing, but by loathing.

You'd think such hate would be enough to make me cut ties with the weasel. Well, even hate keeps the fire of emotion alive. True freedom comes only when the flames die out completely – when there is absolutely nothing left to feel at all.

But let's not jump the gun.

I probably should jump off the soapbox, though.

*attempts elegant jump*

At that point, my entire life was focused on him: on everything he had done, hadn't done, was doing, wasn't doing, and might one day do or not do.

It was eating me up inside, and my daughter – now old enough and astute enough to realise when something wasn't flying straight – could see it.

In the time that I was with Tinder Guy, she really wasn't herself.

I'd moved her away from the only home and family she'd ever known. I'd shoved her into a completely new environment (with shitty flooring and other fittings), and some guy had taken away her mum.

She witnessed the tears and distress. She was privy to the angst and the heartache when I was his second (or, for all I knew, his third, or fourth) choice.

While she was heading into preteens and dealing with her own fluctuating hormones and rollercoaster of emotions, I was focused on my own emotions; my own rollercoaster.

I cringe when I think of the shit that she had to behold.

She asked about the nightmares that made me cry in my sleep.

She asked me what she needed to do to make me happy.

I was a fucking arsehole.

Contemptible though I was, I did try my best to protect her from the worst of the shit storm I was riding out with Tinder Guy. She slept over at my parents one night a week, so I tried to reserve the most vehement fighting and fuckery for those nights. She did, however, have first-hand experience of my inability to function in polite society. I no longer remembered school projects or excursions. I forgot to draw money for yet another fucking donation. I failed to sign homework and read school notices. I was late getting out of bed, and late getting her to school. There were days when I walked about in the kind of daze you'd associate with the zombie apocalypse, where I'd drive her to school in eerie silence and then spend the hours at my desk counting down the minutes until I could drive past Tinder Guy's house to catch him out.

My life became a practiced art of just getting through one day at a time. I was barely surviving. All my passionate feelings about parenting took a back seat to my need for his love.

I managed to get by by doing the bare minimum, and not a thing more. My daughter was still fed, dressed in clean clothes, and slept in a house which was clean (enough) and warm. I never defaulted on school fees or rent. It wasn't as though I was getting stupidly drunk or slitting my wrists.

I hadn't completely lost it yet.

Wait.

It gets better.

Tinder Guy still maintained that we were exclusive. That there was no one else.

Yet the second toothbrush had become a permanent feature in his bathroom. A new, purple detangling comb now sat next to the chick-scented shampoo in the shower.

I still took the occasional trip past his house on my lunchbreak.

Only occasionally.

If I was in the area.

Or less than 50 km away.

I wrestled with the obvious truth, and with the hope that I was wrong. That perhaps the other car in the driveway just belonged to a friend, or that everyone needed a backup toothbrush, or that he had arbitrarily developed a love for frangipani-scented shampoo. And needed a detangling comb for his crew cut.

The time came, eventually, when the feeble voice of my subconscious belief managed to penetrate my delusion-induced brain fog. I could no longer con myself. He was cheating on me again, and I became fixated on catching him in the act.

Since he wasn't prepared to give up the lie, and because my current line of subtle interrogation clearly wasn't cutting it, I felt I had little choice but to rely on my keen investigative prowess to obtain evidence to hurt myself just enough that I would finally be able to leave. I needed cold, hard proof of his cheating before I could get my heart to catch up with what my mind already knew.

As if the hurt I'd endured so far wasn't enough.

I mean, what did I really need to happen? Did I need to physically see him thrusting his way into somebody else?

One day, the opportunity to investigate presented itself. When I tried to call him to arrange to drop off his spare house keys, he red-phoned me. About ten minutes later, he texted to let me know that I could bring them by that evening. He was clearly unable to take my call.

Oh, you sly scoundrel, you.

Obviously, I went over to his house immediately.

Understandably.

Because that is the clever, least crazy, thing to do.

I put on my military-grade stealth gear, stuck night-vision goggles on my face, pulled out my two-way radio, shoved a knife in the back of my pants – and one in my boot for good measure – and fastened my nine-mil handgun into its holster.

Okay fine, I didn't dress like a combat soldier.

Also, night-vision goggles in the daytime would have been stupid. A two-way radio, even more so – who the fuck was I planning to communicate with?

And I didn't own a gun. Because I'd probably have shot myself with it.

I didn't take two knives, either.

I didn't even take one knife.

I was afraid of injuring myself. Exactly how do people stick knives in their pants and boots without mutilating themselves, anyway?

Forty minutes later, I barged through his front door, intending to catch him in the act.

It would have been a truly marvellous story for future dinner parties, had I managed to kick the fucking door down.

Just imagine: 'The thing came right off its hinges! Badass that I am.'

Alas, I simply opened the door with the keys I was supposed to return.

In what was probably the first anticlimax in that house that day, there was no acting in which to catch anyone.

The place was deserted, and it reeked of sex.

In the bedroom, his duvet was thrown on the carpet (he could afford carpet).

Who the fuck doesn't make their Goddamned bed, I thought. I sneered in disgust at yet another one of his shortcomings. The list was getting longer and longer.

Then I saw the fresh stains on the mattress.

Every single atom within me was screaming to pummel him. I wanted him to pay for the heartache and turmoil he was causing me. I wanted him to sit in the filth and shit that he had created. I wanted him to feel even an ounce of the hurt, rejection, and betrayal that I was feeling as a result of his horrendous behaviour. More than anything, though, I wanted him to recognise how much he was breaking me.

I'd never felt the need to snoop through his things before. Or perhaps I'd been scared of what I might find. But now, finally, the need to know the truth overrode the need to delude myself.

It's funny how, when you're possessed by searing, white-hot anger, a sense of ominous calm falls over you.

I began casing the joint.

Calmly.

Methodically.

I rifled through his room.

I dug through the dustbins.

I rummaged through drawers.

I went through the contents of the fridge.

Because that is what you do when you are desperate.

Things that, had you seen someone else doing, you would be calling an asylum.

To get them off the streets.

To protect the rest of humanity from their lunacy.

Selfless humanitarian that you are.

Perhaps someone should have called an asylum to come and get me from Tinder Guy's house. I had become straight-up loony.

Faulty as fuck.

The desperation that gripped me evidently overshadowed my ability to think clearly, and my focus became solely on revenge.

I found our sex toys in an overnight bag that never came with him to my house. They had clearly been utilised. There were used condoms in the bedroom bin. There was a pair of size 0 knickers (do I need to reiterate that these were much smaller than mine?) under the bed. With every new piece of evidence that I found, I cracked a little more.

With glazed eyes (could have used those goggles) and a broken heart, I took all these things out of their hidey holes and placed them – for his perusal – on an impeccably made bed, along with a note (okay, an essay).

Amazing how it took me an entire essay to say, 'Fuck you, motherfucker!'

I left with a hole in my heart the size of the Great Karoo and zero sense of my own identity, and with the intention of never coming back.

But intentions aren't always enough. You know what they say about the road to hell...

Maybe shooting myself would have been simpler.
