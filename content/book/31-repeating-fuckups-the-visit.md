# The Visit

In preparation for my trip to visit the Afrikaner in his home in a private estate in Cape Town, I bought a ton of special outfits.

Naturally, I also needed a new suitcase to accommodate said outfits: 20 pairs of shoes (for everything from running a marathon to walking a runway), five jackets (as we coasted into the heat of African summer), 12 pairs of jeans (living on the edge trying to get away with the bare minimum), 15 handbags, and a range of lingerie for every conceivable whim. I packed clothes for sexy dates, clothes for lounging around, clothes for the beach, and clothes for going to the opera. (He hadn't expressly said that he liked classical music, but a man of his calibre was bound to have such refined taste. Also, it was Cape Town, and people did pretentious shit there, so you never knew. I figured it was best to be prepared.)

I packed clothes for scorching heat. Clothes in case there was a blizzard. Clothes in case there was an apocalypse. Clothes in case I met his parents. Clothes in case the Top Billing team unexpectedly showed up to do a shoot at his home. Clothes sufficient that, were I stranded on Robben Island for eight weeks, I wouldn't need a washing machine.

I feel like this is an opportune time for a soapbox segment. Heck, I don't even need a soapbox for this one – I could simply turn my suitcase on its side and hop on up!

This is dedicated to all the men out there who don't understand why women pack so many clothes 'just in case' when going on holiday.

Use your damned logic.

It's in the name.

It is a case.

You just pack said clothes in said case.

'Clothes just in case.'

Voila.

*dismounts and sets case right way up*

The night before my scheduled departure, although I had set 20 alarms to wake me up in time, I was convinced I would sleep through all of them. Consequently, I did not sleep at all.

Great.

As if I didn't have enough bags to lug around, I now had to add haemorrhoid cream to my growing stash of travel supplies – for the bags under my eyes. (Seriously, it works wonders for this! I've not yet had occasion to use it on my starfish, so I can't comment on the effectiveness of its intended use, but it works a charm for reducing swelling elsewhere.)

The Afrikaner would be picking me up from the airport, so I needed to ensure that my first impression of walking out of the terminal was a lasting one – in the good way. I wore a pair of new dark-wash jeans with an equally new pink, button-up peplum top (I do love them peplum tops) and matching pink sneakers. My roots were redone, my legs were smooth, the downtown hedges had been professionally landscaped, I had a French manicure, and I felt unreasonably exhilarated.

It was one thing spending time in guesthouses; it was another thing entirely being welcomed into someone's home.

When someone invites you into their space, you get a sense of the type of person they truly are.

It also happens that you start to picture yourself in that space, making it your own.

No?

Just me?

Okay, then.

I had told my family that no decision was made and that the trip was purely exploratory, but by the time I walked through his front door, I had already made up my heart, if not my mind. My heart was set on him.

Oops.

Shit.

I was in trouble.

The Cape was beautiful. Although we didn't travel the coast and I wasn't able to post any #beachvibes on social media, we spent time in the winelands around Paarl and Stellenbosch. We went to beautiful, #bespoke restaurants, ate #organic food, and sipped stunning red blends while eating mini chocolates at #sunset.

Every moment was easy.

We spoke easily, we laughed easily, we navigated each other easily.

We had complex conversations without it feeling overbearing.

The sex was also easy. I wouldn't say that it was off-the-charts exciting, but it was good. It was simple. It was safe, secure, and meaningful.

At least it was to me.

Another soapbox moment, if I may.

Some guys seem to think that, to please a woman, they have to re-enact the Kama Sutra (or, worse, Porn Hub). They also think that they have to randy on for hours, employing an arsenal of kinky techniques to keep our interest.

While I'm not knocking the passionate kind of sex I'd had with Tinder Guy, it honestly can get a bit much. There are only so many nights a week that one can be completely spent and undone and still function (and walk normally) the next day.

It doesn't always have to be 'lust or bust'.

In contrast to what erotic literature would have you believe, wild, adventurous sex is really only fun for the first hour or so. After that, chasing orgasms grows laborious. Stamina wears down. Moisture dries up. And suddenly you'd rather be listening to your kid massacre the 28-hour recital of their day.

Pity.

When we weren't busy having a satisfying time in the sack, I learnt new things about the Afrikaner that endeared him to me even more.

I loved that he wanted to be on time for things. Like 'oh-my-God-pick-an-outfit-already' on time.

Men.

They just don't get it.

Hand me back that soapbox.

Men are blessed with the simplicity of choosing a single shirt and a pair of pants (usually the ones right on top of the clean laundry pile). Some men don't even wear undies.

Bloody free-ballers.

It is entirely different for women.

Clothing considerations need to be made about patterns (always vertical lines, never horizontal). About colours (never white on cream). About the current weather. About the potential weather. About the time of the month. Women must worry about being overdressed. About being underdressed. About even wearing a dress (especially if there is the possibility of strong winds).

It's a science.

My usual dressing routine starts with me lying in bed in my birthday suit for an hour after a shower while I contemplate what to wear. Once I have devised an outfit – and with a scant 30 minutes to spare before I need to leave the house – I start getting dressed.

Only to find that, although I have picked a light-pink blouse and white skinny jeans, all my pale bras are in the wash and, thanks to a surprise visit of my recurrent tummy bloat, I cannot button up the jeans. Also, who am I kidding? I can't wear white jeans. They proudly display every curve – and all the hail damage. I don't know why I bought them in the first place.

Wishful thinking at its prime.

So, back to the drawing board.

I then work up an almighty sweat digging through the contents of my cupboard (which contains about 200 million articles of clothing, and not a single thing that is suitable to wear). The sweatiness makes it almost impossible to pull on the go-to black leggings I have had to settle on, even though I wore a similar pair yesterday, and the day before that. And this is all before I've even noticed that the lace-free sneakers (which I was planning to shove on my feet in record time) have a giant mark on them and can't possibly be worn out.

Back to the drawing board again – with five minutes till I need to leave.

Fuckity fuck.

The routine was no different at the Afrikaner's house.

To make matters worse, since I obviously couldn't poo at his house, my belly was brutally bloated. Naturally, none of the outfits that I packed accommodated the distended tummy, and although I had packed enough 'in case' clothing to sink a warship, I hadn't packed a single pair of go-to black leggings.

Bugger.

The Afrikaner evidently had no problem with his bowels.

Each morning, he'd disappear into the bathroom for about two hours. Then he'd eat a banana, make coffee, and play with his dog, while I lay bloated in bed trying not to explode.

Love is patient.

Love is constipated.

I found it so cute how he crossed his legs while reading or watching the news, too. He looked so serious when he did this.

He turned over awkwardly in bed, and I loved it. I loved it mostly because he made me feel totally graceful by comparison. I sort of turned over in increments. He just did the whole thing in one fell swoop. Lucky he didn't have a water bed, or I'd have been flung off.

I found it super endearing when he was a teeny bit insecure about his beard regrowth and whether it was scratching me when he kissed me.

He was in the midst of redecorating his house when I came to visit, and he asked for my input on everything. I chose furniture and colours to suit different rooms, and he seemed genuinely keen on my feedback and choices.

Do you know how exciting it is when someone includes you in these sorts of decisions? It feels like they're asking you your opinion because they are imagining you in that space too. I mean, he might just have been asking because he knew I had exceptional taste in décor (well, at least, I think I do). Or he could be asking because he wanted me to move in with him and have his babies and stuff.

I was doing the cool cucumber thing again.

Although it wasn't yet December, everywhere we went had already put up their festive season decorations. Extravagant Christmas trees greeted us at the entrances to shopping malls. Tinsel adorned the window frames of the charming restaurants he took me to. I was spoilt with great nights out, amazing food, and exceptional wine – and the festivity added to the magic of it all.

Before I knew it, the trip had come to an end. And, with a phone full of joy-sparking, heart-warming photos, with my jeans' top button unfastened (because I still hadn't had a poo), with a memory bank overflowing and with a heart full of warm fuzzy feelings, I was off to the airport to make my trip home.

As the plane took off, and the romance faded into the clouds behind me, I found the nearest airplane bathroom, pressed a wad of toilet paper against my bum, and finally let out a week's worth of gas.

Magical trip? Definitely.

Dignified ending? Not quite.
