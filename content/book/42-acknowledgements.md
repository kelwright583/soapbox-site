# Acknowledgements

This book would not exist without Lauren Shapiro: author, editor, writing coach, comma whisperer, and (occasionally) de facto therapist.

Working with Lauren wasn't just about the manuscript; it was like an edit of my soul.

Word by word, she helped me put both my sentences and myself back together. She taught me how to write the hard stuff without flinching, and how to cut the fluff (even when it was wrapped in sentimental blankets).

Thank you for sitting with me – literally and metaphorically – for over three years, holding space for both the writer I was, and the woman I was becoming during the process. And for gently (sometimes not so gently) steering me away from the emotional equivalent of writing in Comic Sans.

To my parents,

Thank you for standing by me when I had absolutely no idea what I was doing (even as I vehemently protested that I did).

Thank you for footing the bill when I couldn't even spell 'gynaecologist', for showing up when you could have looked away, and for loving my daughter like she'd always been yours.

There are girls who don't get to choose to keep their babies; the older I get, the more I realise what a rare, radical privilege it was that I did. You made that choice possible. For that I will be forever grateful.

Also, thank you in advance for your grace (and sense of humour) when you read this.

Especially the filthy language. Yes, I was raised better. But no, I'm not sorry. Your daughter has a mouth on her – but then, you raised her to speak her truth.

To my daughter,

Watching you grow has been my biggest joy – from banging and clanging with pots and spoons to performing on stage with pianos and flutes. Supporting you has been my second biggest joy – from finding glitter for school projects to finding you funding for international choir tours. If you ever need a kidney, I would find that for you, too!

Thank you for teaching me patience – not just with kids, by with myself – and the real meaning of unconditional love. Thank you also for finally letting me go to the bathroom by myself (and, one day, maybe even leaving my expensive toiletries alone).

Darling, you are the mirror of my growth, reflecting both my worst and best traits back at me. My life truly wouldn't be as rich without you.

You were the plot twist no one saw coming, and the punchline that made it all worth telling.
