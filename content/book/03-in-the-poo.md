# In The Poo

Mine felt more like a bundle of 'what the actual fuck do I do now?'

I needed to change her nappy (which I had no fricking idea how to do), and rather than contemplating the job, I was staring – unseeing – at my baby, only a day old.

Brand, spanking new.

She was a perfect, little, squirming human, sprawled out on my hospital bed. I was watching her suck little blisters onto her tiny hand. A hand that looked exactly like a mini version of mine.

It was her perfect nails that were the centre of my attention.

Frankly, her nails were the only thing I could focus on. Everything else was a blur.

Teeny, tiny, perfect little nails. Nails which she grew all on her own.

This was where it started.

Actually, it started nine months prior, when I also chose to sprawl myself out on a bed (not the hospital kind). And no, I wasn't sucking my hand. Nope, I chose to do things that mummies and daddies do, when they are married, and love each other, and have good jobs and financial security, and life experience, and health insurance, and a semblance of how they plan to feed and clothe their bundle of whatever.

Gosh darn it, what the hell have I done? My mind raged at me.

It must be in your 20s that you get to know stuff, I figured.

Stuff like how to get the sticky tar poo off your baby's very cute, very little bottom without scraping her skin right down to the hypodermis, and without having her scream blue murder due to the frigid hospital (both in temperature and in atmosphere) because you aren't doing it fast enough.

You don't know this stuff when you are 17.

I certainly didn't have the foggiest notion how to do it. I didn't know how to change a nappy, or breastfeed, or how to hold my baby carefully so that her tiny head didn't fall off her little body and plummet accusingly to the floor.

I had no idea how to place my baby back in her crib after the 57th failed attempt at breastfeeding. The nurses had told me that I needed to lay her on her side, but I battled to prop her up just right, so that she didn't promptly roll onto her face and succumb to death by asphyxiation. I definitely could not have contemplated placing her on her tummy, head turned at a right angle, like some mums were able to do.

I'd have snapped her little neck.

I didn't have the vaguest idea how I would navigate my newfound 'mum' status. All I knew was that I desperately wanted – no, needed – my own mum.

But my mum couldn't do this for me. I needed to do it myself.

And so, there we were, my new baby and me. She sucking, and I sucking at it, in the midst of the poo, wondering how the hell we had gotten there.
