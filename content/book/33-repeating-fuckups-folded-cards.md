# Folded Cards

My daughter and I were playing a game of Uno on my bed before her bedtime. We were laughing and joking – now a regular part of our everyday interaction. I was focused on being present with her. Focused on dismissing thoughts of the Afrikaner. Focused on not losing yet another hand to her, since she is a cunning, ruthless, no-limits soldier in a card game.

A text alert jerked my attention from the tenth hand that she was winning to my phone screen, which flashed with a message from him: 'I need to tell you first.'

I was so shocked that I dropped my cards.

It had been a month since the Afrikaner had told me he wanted to 'take it slow'. A month since I had heard his voice. Evidently, it was a month since he'd given me any thought.

My daughter threw me a look (no doubt waiting to pounce on my careless mistake of showing my hand).

As I scrabbled to gather my cards, I surreptitiously leant over to have a closer look at the screen.

A second message popped up: 'I met someone in December, and I am giving it a chance officially.'

Huh?

Transferring my cards to one trembling hand, I picked up the phone with the other.

I read that short message again and again and again.

How magnanimous. He'd thought to tell me first.

I'd had my suspicions about this for a while, so the message wasn't a total surprise.

What was a surprise, though, was that I could not – if you paid me – have associated that kind of carelessness or thoughtlessness with him. I could not conceive that someone who was mature, wise, gentle, and experienced – someone who knew the bullshit I'd been through in prior relationships – could have thought me so unimportant that he could send me a text, out of the blue, with such significant information, with no apparent thought for my wellbeing.

And while I was being thrashed at Uno, no less.

Fine, maybe our relationship wouldn't have worked out anyway.

The distance thing was shit. The language thing, also. The age thing might have been playing on his mind. The fact that I was a single mum was probably also an issue. Maybe even the money disparity was something he was worried about.

I get it.

I'm not delusional.

Except for that one time when I thought I would, like, move my whole life across the country so I could date someone and have his babies, after only meeting him twice.

Okay, maybe a little delusional.

But back to my point...

What he did smarted; how he did it cut me to the core.

Incidentally, I am 100% down with people changing their minds.

It's a human right. Love is tricky. It's easy to fall in and out of it. It's possible to have a change of heart, since our hearts often do their own things, despite ourselves. My tolerance for people changing their minds is fairly high.

However, my tolerance for people who think it's okay to lead me astray when they have no intention of commitment is low (and getting lower the older I get).

And my tolerance for people who think it is appropriate to drop a text out of nowhere, telling me they met – and are now in a relationship with – someone else, only a few weeks after they were talking about our future together while he caressed my nipples in bed, is zero.

Do better, big guy.

If you change your mind, there are ways to communicate it.

Maybe you can write me an essay. Is essay-writing a Love Language?

What about an improvised platform used by a self-appointed genius to wax lyrical about all the rights and wrongs of relational communication?

Soapboxes are typically used to address a crowd. This time, however, I'm going to use it to metaphorically look the Afrikaner square in the eye (did I mention he was tall?) and give him a piece of my mind. The fact that the world will hear my grievances is a bonus.

Dude, you are a grown man.

A fully functional, intelligent, astute, successful man.

Pick up the fucking phone!

And if you must text, let it be one that reads, 'When are you able to talk?'

If you knew weeks ago that there was someone else, don't bullshit me with 'I'm keen to take it slow.' Have some semblance of integrity and say, 'I'm keen to see who else is out there.'

And don't slam me with the news without warning – for all you know, you could be interrupting special time with my kid, who's clutching her cards, patiently waiting on my next move (so that she can counter and decimate me completely).

You could be putting me in a position where I must hold back tears in front of my daughter in the middle of a card game, trying to pass off my misery as a result of losing to her (again!).

She's not stupid.

She wasn't buying it.

The worst part was that you had this change of heart after my visit to your home. It made me question whether it was your getting to know me that changed your mind.

It would have been really great if I'd been afforded the courtesy of an exit interview. I could've walked into your fancy-ass office in full peplum powerwear, sipping a smug decaf almond-milk latte, and asked why someone else was – again! – chosen over me.

Was it the cellulite? The stretchmarks? The bloating?

Was it because of my sailor mouth? I don't remember swearing that fucking much!

Was it because I tucked my dress into my knickers to frolic in the Magalies River? I'll grant that it wasn't exactly Queen Victoria's idea of ladylike behaviour, but was it enough to break off a relationship?

Ah shit. Was it because I nearly killed you when we went tandem quad biking? I only wanted to flaunt what an excellent rider I was. I didn't mean to nearly kill you; I was just really excited to show you that I could lurch over that boulder at full tilt. I thought you would hang on.

Should I have just chosen chicken nuggets? I could have learnt to be more excited about chicken nuggets. And less excited about pizza or hamburgers or medium-rare steak. Or lamb. Oooh, or sushi.

Deep-fried calamari?

But definitely, I could have gotten keen on chicken nuggets.

Was it because I got a regular French manicure, rather than the ombre French that you said you liked, 'on your cousin'?

Was it because I couldn't satisfy you?

Well, had I had the opportunity, I would have thrown that decaf almond-milk latte in your mature, intelligent, successful, handsome face!

You hurt me, you prick.

You broke my heart!

'Mum?' my daughter's sweet voice called through the malaise.

Then I folded.

And not just my cards.

My hope, my certainty, my self-worth – it all slipped from my fingers like the Uno hand I'd been clutching.
