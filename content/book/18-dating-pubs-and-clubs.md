# Pubs and Clubs

When you have a kid, dating is challenging. I was now a package deal, and that was often more than most men had bargained for.

Given that I didn't get much relationship experience in school (except for the one that resulted in a baby), I didn't really know where to start looking for one.

There weren't many opportunities for me to meet people. And if an opportunity did present itself, I didn't really have the option of taking things further to get to know the prospective partner without having to organise childcare and tactics to avoid questions from my parents (or my daughter) before I had the answers.

It was a Catch-22 situation: I wanted to get to know someone separately from my child, so that I could formulate an opinion as to whether they were actually a paedophile or kidnapper. But it would be difficult to gauge whether they would be a good fit with my daughter without introducing them to her.

I wanted her to be open to the idea of me dating. I wanted her to understand that I was allowed to have a life independent of her, and that my relationships – until I decided otherwise – could remain private. At the same time, I wanted her to feel included and welcome in all aspects of my life, and to have exposure to what positive, healthy relationships looked like.

Basically, dating was a walk in the park.

Jurassic Park.

Finding a suitable man wasn't easy. The men who were functional, independent, intelligent, and emotionally astute had already been snatched up by well-educated Lip Gloss Ladies, who were now #stayathomemoms, living a #bespoke life (probably with a constant headache).

It seemed like the only men available were T-rexes. Their short arms couldn't reach their wallets when it was important, and their big teeth had no trouble chewing you up and spitting you out after they'd had their fill.

Going back to my old stomping grounds at the pubs was disastrous. The guys there tended to be in varying stages of divorce, so damn shabby, and all kinds of drunk. Most were also nearly twice my age. And it showed – especially in their dance moves: arms akimbo, a step-and-tap if they were feeling adventurous, maybe with a little finger-click thrown in now and then.

If someone was kind enough to offer me a drink, it would probably have been a brandy and Coke (probably the cheap brandy), and it probably would have ended up down my top after having been forced on me by a grubby man in his earnest quest to have me throw it back like a scrumhalf in premier league rugby.

A real turn-on.

In the bathroom, trying to salvage my top, I'd invariably have to deal with a tear-streaked woman who couldn't handle her booze and was heartbroken that last Saturday's flavour (whom she'd come back to see this Saturday) was now stepping and tapping the night away with another high-hemline-flaunting, ring-on-every-finger-but-one, blue-eyelinered divorcee.

Clubs were also out of the question. Firstly, I could no longer fit into miniskirts and crop tops without risking undesirable body parts making spontaneous cameo appearances. Secondly, I had no idea how to dance in those types of settings anymore. I had once been pretty confident (after enough tequila), but I now felt awkward doing the sexy-hands-in-the-air, fingers-gliding-down-my-body thing on the dance floor.

The blue-eyelinered divorcees looked pretty sensual doing that, while I just felt like, 'Why the heck have I got my hands in the air?'

'What am I reaching for?'

'Why am I touching my boobs? I don't want to risk dislodging them. Do you know how long it took me to get them securely in this bra, all facing the same way?'

Everyone else seemed to know how to pull off the sexy-hands thing.

But when I put my hands in the air to dance, I looked like an air hostess making her inflight safety announcements.

'Ladies and gentlemen, I'd like to direct your attention to the DJ. We now request a few minutes of your complete attention for our very important dancing demonstration.'

nts nts nts...

'When the disco lights illuminate, you must fasten your belt. If you are not too drunk yet, insert the metal tip into the buckle until you hear a click, and tighten by pulling on the loose end of the strap.'

*awkward hand gestures across my stomach region*

nts nts nts...

whicky whicky...

'To release your belt, lift the upper portion of the buckle.'

*clicking vigorously*

'We suggest that you keep your belt fastened throughout the dance, as we may experience some turbulence.'

*booty-hopping rendition of twerking*

nts nts nts...

'There are several emergency exits in this club, which are being pointed out to you now.'

*points both hands to the front. points both hands to the back. points to the floor. points to the stairs*

'Please take a moment to locate your nearest exit, bearing in mind that it might be behind you. If we need to evacuate the club, floor-level lighting will guide you towards the exits. Alternatively, simply follow the harrowing screams, and try not to fall down the stairs.'

whicky whicky whicky...

Have you noticed that there are always stairs at a jol? Don't they realise that people leave these places when they can no longer see straight? How are they supposed to navigate such an outlandish death trap when they can't locate – never mind feel – their feet? And don't even get me started on high heels!

So, with pubs and clubs off the cards, church might have been a place to meet people.

Except that it wasn't.

I didn't feel the need to be prayed for on my first date, or to be offered conditional grace (I could get it unconditionally at home, without having to wake up at some ridiculously stupid hour on a Sunday, when I should have been sleeping off my cheap brandy).

Christian men also tended to prefer their women pure and virginal.

Oh well. So much for that, then.

Maybe I could have gotten lucky. Some of these Christian men might have been benevolent and charitable, with have a fondness for picking up sullied women and saving them from eternal damnation.

So kind.

Perhaps meeting someone spectacular while grocery shopping would have been more promising.

However, apparently, following Mr Hottie home from Woolworths and sneaking into his house unannounced is frowned upon.

Pity.

Honestly, if I make you an orgasmically delicious dinner and lay it out with wine and candles, don't give me this 'Who are you, and how did you get in my house?' bullshit.

Ungrateful jerk.

It appeared that the options for meeting someone who was functional, independent, intelligent, emotionally astute, and mature enough to take on a package deal were slim to none.

Feeling rather lonely and desperate for a good, solid relationship that clearly wasn't materialising, I began to wonder whether it was because there was something wrong with me.

I'd tried humour.

I'd tried strategising.

I'd tried rationalising.

I'd tried denial.

I'd tried brandy.

I'd tried dancing.

I'd tried touching my boobs.

I'd tried high heels (never again).

I'd tried stalking random hotties in Woolies (just the once).

But when everyone else seemed to be pairing off and I was still fielding cartoon-themed memes from the class text message group, the silence started to get really loud.

Clearly, my meet-cute was running late.

Probably stuck in traffic. Behind a convoy of Lip Gloss Ladies.

It took all my maturity and self-compassion to convince myself that, maybe, the reason I hadn't yet met someone wasn't because something was wrong with me. Maybe it was just because I'd spent a few years raising a tiny human, working multiple jobs, pretending to love bake sales, and being repeatedly ghosted by the Tooth Fairy.

Maybe I simply hadn't had time to meet someone. At least, not someone worth introducing to my daughter.

Still, it would've been nice to have a hand to hold that wasn't sticky with tomato sauce.
