# The Perfect Storm that is Teenage Pregnancy and Motherhood

## Boys

Usually, the story of the teenage mum takes the shape of a wayward child whose family is descended from the other side of the tracks, or of a family where culture is limited to dairy, and etiquette is reserved for funerals.

But that's not my story at all.

I wasn't some social outlier. I was a typical daughter of regular parents and belonged to a normal family.

My family was of average financial status. We were well off enough that I was privileged to attend one of Durban's better schools, which boasted exceptional facilities and academic results, but we weren't so wealthy that we could go on overseas holidays, or have the latest cars, or even the full bouquet of DSTV (there was no such thing as Netflix or Amazon Prime back then).

Growing up, while good at many things, I was never truly excellent at anything. I could draw. I could paint. I could sing. I had ball skills. I could study and get relatively good grades. But never was I noticed for anything in particular.

I was, and still am, the proverbial Jack of all trades and master of none.

I think I'd be considered pretty – in the same way I'd imagine any ordinary Jane would be (with the obvious exception of Jane in Pride and Prejudice). But no one's going to be dropping dead on account of my gorgeousness.

At school, I was good at sports – but, while I often made the A teams, I warmed the bench more than I played.

Although I scored well in tests, I would never have been considered summa-cum-laude material.

I wasn't geeky enough to be in the 'nerd crew', nor was I cool enough to be friends with the popular crowd.

My family was average.

My experiences were average.

I was average.

The only thing that was not average was my relationship with boys.

I may perhaps have been considered 'girly' in that I had long, blonde hair. And, well, a vagina. So, yes, technically girly.

But I was never flirty enough, skinny enough, or stupid enough to compete with the girls whom I referred to as the Lip Gloss Ladies.

You know the ones. They use that yucky, cheap, runny, sweet, glossy rubbish on their lips. That fruity-flavoured, roll-on KY Jelly which you can buy in bulk at some dreadful, dodgy Chinese shop.

Their outfit of choice was usually a plummeting, V-neck, tight top which exposed the edges of a lacy bra (while they hoped no one would see the socks which were stuffed inside).

I was never prepared to dumb myself down (to below average) to gain attention, and I wasn't prepared to fan any person's ego by being well versed in all things distressed or damsel.

Incidentally, I was prepared to have a good old rant about other girls' shortfalls. (Maybe a soapbox would have come in handy here, though I doubt my fellow teens would have listened to a word I said, and I might have gotten detention for blocking traffic in the school hallways.)

I received no romantic attention at all from boys, and held fast in my role as friend, sidekick, third wheel, and matchmaker.

During high school, I had two crushes. One was on a set of identical twins (I couldn't choose between them, and so they will be hereafter communally referred to as Twin); the other was on my bestie, who, if I'm being truthful, definitely wouldn't have considered me his best friend (at least I was not without a good imagination).

Having gone to an all-girls high school, I'd met these boys at church. I'd befriended them at the church youth centre and spent time with them on church outings.

I had a crush on them for what felt like ages. None of these halfwits considered me girlfriend material, though. And so, it transpired that – up until age 15 – I'd not so much as held a boy's hand, never kissed a guy, and certainly had never got to any kind of base with them (softball or other).

Given my Christian upbringing, I didn't think this was such a bad thing. In those years, I was pretty intent – in theory – on remaining beyond reproach and conducting myself in what I thought were the ways the church and my folks would have me conduct myself.

I do remember, though, feeling at odds with my desires for wanting a boyfriend (or at least wanting to be noticed by boys), and also wanting desperately to please my folks. Therefore, I maintained that I would be single (while simultaneously praying for a boyfriend).

It wasn't confusing at all.

Twin once sent me a text asking if I would join him and 'the boys' at the local skating rink. It only took an entire day of begging to convince my parents to let me go. They gave their go-ahead on the proviso that they would take and fetch me from the rink (and no doubt hang around like stalkers while we were there).

Another condition was that I attend church that Sunday. This was hardly a challenging negotiation, given that Twin and Bestie would be at church too. At least if we kissed at the rink or did anything scandalous, we could pray about it the next day.

Hopefully while holding hands. Intimately.

The excitement lasted about as long as my pocket money did: all of two minutes. (Funny, nothing's changed with my salary today.)

I received a further text from Twin, asking if I could invite one of the Lip Gloss Ladies to the rink so that he could hook up with her.

This wasn't unlike any other occasion where I was invited out by 'the boys'.

There were several occasions when I was pulled into their confidence so that they might get someone's phone number from me, or get an idea of what chocolate they should buy for one of my girlfriends, or ask me how to fold a heart out of a letter they'd written for one of these girls.

I was never the girl who held a boy's attention outside of the confines of the Friend Zone. As a result of this, I reckon, I was desperate to be noticed by the opposite sex, and would accept attention from anyone who would stumble across my path.

At around 15, I met my daughter's father/sperm donor (AKA SD). He was five years my senior, and he took an interest in me. He evidently had matured past Lip Gloss Ladies with their perky little boobs, and had acquired a taste for naked-lipped, C-cupped, average birds.

Falling for him was easy. Letting go of the fantasy that Twin or Bestie might fall madly in love with me? Also easy, because after years of crushing on them, our relationship had not undergone rezoning. Choosing SD meant that I finally moved away from simply being a pal, and that I now had someone to kiss and hold hands with.

Someone who was old enough to drive me to the ice rink himself.

Old enough to pick me up from school

Old enough to introduce me to Smirnoff Spin.

Old enough to completely obliterate all my plans to stay a virgin until marriage.

Old enough to take the course of my life and point it directly toward the Bermuda Triangle – where plans, dreams, and common sense all mysteriously vanished.

He did all of this because I was young enough to provide him with my unwavering consent, consequences be damned.

Wise.

I sometimes wish I could go back in time and punch my younger self in my decidedly average face.

My parents were less than thrilled about my relationship with SD. An age gap of five years in one's teens was significant, and I think that the concern was that I was not emotionally ready for that kind of commitment. With my limited exposure to men outside of church, they obviously feared that I was not cut out to protect myself and my innocence in a mature relationship. As a result, they restricted all contact with him to supervised visits and continued to encourage me to throw myself at school, sports, social gatherings, and all things beginning with 's' – with the exception of sex.

They did not endorse sex at all.

I had known, I think, that SD would never be a permanent thing, and hindsight (now more hind, less sight) would have it that he perhaps should never have been my partner.

I was so enamoured with the notion that I had a boyfriend, that I lost my ability to make clear and well-thought-out decisions.

It wasn't that I was forced into sex. He always behaved like a gentleman. At least, what I thought passed for gentlemanly at 16.

Now, of course, I know better. But I think that in my quest to feel more than average, I was somewhat coerced into feeling as though sex was something I would like to do.

Also, saying 'no' was not so easy while, in comparison, getting hot and moist was.

Much like when it gets into walls and roofs, it turns out that moisture is as much of a concern when it gets into teenage girls.

Crude, I know. But so was what happened next.
