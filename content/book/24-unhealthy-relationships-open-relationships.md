# The Thing about Open Relationships

He looked me dead in the eye.

'My relationship with her? It's not serious.'

'Basically over.'

'Just tying up loose ends.'

I convinced myself that all I needed to do was wait it out.

It was so close.

Within arm's reach.

Just over that next hill.

It only took about a year.

He evidently had a lot of loose ends.

Until then, I would have to be satisfied with an 'open' relationship.

Open. As in: his pants, the door, and my emotional wound.

This is, as I learnt, part of the modern phenomenon known as hook-up culture. I'm sure the practice has existed for millennia (only, it was called 'being loose'); however, these days, educated and trusted mental health specialists like psychologists recognise and sometimes even advocate for it.

It's true. I googled it.

You can't make this shit up.

While it caused me heartache, I somehow rationalised to myself that sharing him with someone else was less painful than not having him in my life at all. I convinced myself that, as long as I was knew what was going on, I couldn't be cheated, and therefore I couldn't be hurt. He couldn't stab me in the back if I was facing the situation head-on. If this was a test in life, I figured that going into the classroom prepared was better than not being prepared, or not going into the classroom at all.

Unless it was a trigonometry class, and a test in sin, tan, and cos.

God, I hated trigonometry! I despised those ratios. I thought it was unforgivable to expect 16-year-olds to understand them.

But back to Tinder Guy.

It wasn't all bad. The sex got better and better, and we became closer and closer. We spent whatever time we had available – sorry, whatever time he had available – together.

I met him most days for lunch, and we still had two evenings a week just for us.

He even used the 'L' word.

That's when I knew it was real.

He loved me.

He said it.

Love covers a multitude of sins, so I reckoned I could forgive him (not merely pay lip service – the other chick was probably doing that already). I was so certain that what we had was real, that I was prepared to look past his unforgiveable sins, tans, and cosses.

I became exceptional at eating up his lies.

I threw myself into my newfound hobby of avoiding the truth.

I forced myself to believe that everything was above-board. If I knew he was not exclusive to me, then she must surely have known that he was not exclusive to her, either. I wasn't doing anything wrong, because this was explicitly an open relationship, right?

Right??

While I was waiting for all those loose ends to be tied up, I persuaded myself that I actually preferred the setup of an open relationship. I had the freedom to do what I wanted, when I wanted, with whom I wanted, and I didn't have to answer to anyone.

What I actually wanted was for him to stop seeing her. I wanted him to choose me. To commit to me. Only me.

And I wanted to commit to him.

Only him.

I was quite clearly out of my depth when it came to relationships in general, and perhaps even more inept in my understanding of hook-up culture. But now that I have so much more life experience and wisdom, I have formed very perceptive opinions about both.

Soapbox out!

Alright, I totally get that one shouldn't always settle for the first person one meets, without seeing who else might be out there. I realise that spiking divorce statistics prove that committing to someone before exploring your options could result in a messy ending.

But this hook-up culture shit is the opposite extreme.

It's supposed to give everyone equal freedom and satisfaction. It's supposed to work equally well for all parties involved.

It doesn't.

It's a catalyst for an entire generation of humans who shirk all responsibility for the wellbeing of themselves and others. A generation of humans who are proficient in the art of having their cake (and everyone else's) and eating it, and who are incapable of making a fucking decision and sticking to it because #FOMO.

Hook-up culture means not having to deal with emotional intimacy. But offenders are more than happy to fuck with other people's emotions. They make sure that they say and do just enough to ensure you do not stray. They tell you that you're the one who makes them think that they might 'settle down one day'. And so you are emotionally invested.

Bless.

I have come to the following inalienably common-sense conclusion: open relationships don't work!

Are you hearing this, people??

Inevitably, someone may offer the illusion of commitment, while you remain fastidious in your fidelity to them. And while you dedicate your thoughts, life, and heart to them, they run the streets like a rabid dog in search of the next available bitch on heat.

It's him.

The rabid dog is Tinder Guy.

Just in case you weren't sure.

So that's what happened. I worked around him. He defined the rules, and my life revolved around his availability.

Our open 'relationship' – note how the verts have moved from 'open' to 'relationship' – became an excuse for sex on demand (his demand). He basically treated me like a prostitute. Only a prostitute gets paid for services rendered and doesn't have to lay out money for the date, nor does she have to make the bed and tidy up the house afterward. Oh, didn't I mention? Somewhere along the line, he'd stopped picking up the bill. And the dirty underwear.

He was an expert at giving me just enough attention and interest so that I wouldn't immediately revolt. He gave me adorable nicknames and had conversations with me about the future which sounded a lot like he might one day – if the stars aligned, and it snowed at the equator – commit to me. But this was simply manipulation.

So was his telling me that he loved me.

He understood that he had to say and do certain things to keep me tethered. He told me things to make sure I kept coming back.

'I love you,' he said.

'I'm just not ready to settle down yet,' he said.

'This way is better for everyone,' he said.

The thing about open relationships? They're not progressive – they're bad news with good branding.

Free love was historically famous for freedom from commitment, guilt, and occasionally hygiene. The modern makeover wasn't much better.

Yet I settled for a carefully rationed slice of his affection, and told myself it was enough.

Hey, if you're emotionally starved, even crumbs look good.
