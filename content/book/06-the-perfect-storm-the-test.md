# The Test

Who knew? Putting a condom on a banana (and not on your boyfriend's penis) may result in a baby.

I asked my mum to meet me at a local coffee shop for a delicate conversation to bring her up to speed on my new, delicate condition. I figured that meeting in a public space was a better alternative to a family convention at the dining room table. At a restaurant, the only thing breaking would be the news I was building myself up to tell, and not any unsuspecting crockery.

Throughout my prepared speech, my mother showcased a poker face that could contend with a seasoned gambler. The only hint that she was actually comprehending anything I was saying was that she occasionally swallowed hard. Very hard. As though she had to physically digest the news.

I'd been worried about her reaction, but I hadn't accounted for her heart. Instead of losing her shit and disowning me, she told me that she could never be disappointed in me.

'I'm only upset that this is going to be so hard for you,' she said in her usual calm, nurturing tone. 'I've tried to protect you from adversity your whole life, but here we are. Whatever decisions we make, we will get through this together, as a family.'

Then, my mum (being the ultimate planner and challenge-tackler) booked a pathology appointment at the nearest hospital for the very next day.

I was going for The Test.

The appointment was during the school day, so I had to be granted concession to leave early to attend.

This was the appointment which made it so hard for me to comprehend how nurse outfits have become associated with sexiness.

The nurse, who handed us forms to complete, wore a navy pencil skirt which sat at an odd length. It was the kind of length you'd associate with someone who might be expecting a flood. It wasn't long enough to touch her ankles, but also wasn't short enough to intimate that she may, indeed, actually be a woman.

The severity of her square-toed excuse for a block heel, paired with her too-tight, time-yellowed button-up shirt, as well as the fact that she had the kind of breath that could only have been the byproduct of years of eating vermin, made it certain that I shall never be of the opinion that 'nurse' could be synonymous with anything relating to 'sex', or 'kitten'.

'Wright,' the nurse barked, whilst looking at her clipboard over her ample bosom. 'You're next.'

With legs of lead, I followed her into the torture chamber the sole purpose of which was drawing blood.

'Make a fist,' she commanded through clenched teeth as she looked down at me in my school uniform. She made no attempt at hiding her eyerolls.

After a good tap-tap-tap on the vein in the crease of my arm, she shoved the needle in with a degree of abandon, extracted an alarmingly large amount of blood, and then abruptly handed me a plaster to put on the wound site.

After the nurse had dispossessed me of half of my body's blood volume, I joined my mum back in the waiting room, where we sat in deathly silence, awaiting the results.

If the eyerolling and carelessness had not been indications of the nurse's disdain, the tsk sound she made from her vermin mouth whenever she looked in my direction certainly was.

It felt as though the results of the blood test took forever. This was because I was trying vehemently not to gag at the smell of disinfectant and potpourri, which evidently was used in aid of masking the smell of dead rats.

The bacon and egg toasties which were being sold at the hospital cafeteria down the hall were not a good scent when paired with these hygiene attempts, and I'd recently developed a sense of smell which would have put me in direct competition with an astute canine sniffer. It took every ounce of strength – and very shallow breathing – not to puke all over the waiting room.

In an effort to ignore the rising bile, and to pass the time, I made a conscious decision to think positive thoughts.

Perhaps things would be okay. Maybe the results of the 70 sticks I'd weed on would all be inaccurate?

Positive thoughts, I kept telling myself.

I was positively nauseous.

Positively hormonal.

Positively tender.

Positively pregnant.

Positively fucked.

Or at least I had been, which was the reason that I found myself in this position in the first place.
