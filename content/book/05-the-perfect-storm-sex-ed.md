# Since when do birds fuck bees?

This was a question posed at an event hosted by my high school, whose idea of sex education was to outsource the responsibility to a comedian who presented his interpretation of sexuality to us during an assembly.

It was my only takeaway from the gig.

It is likely that I was so enamoured with this because someone had dared to swear, with such conviction, at a school where candour and expletives were considered the furthest thing from Godliness, and where sex was spoken of only in the confines of a Life Orientation lesson. (Life Orientation is the subject that teaches you about condoms and bananas, but gives you zero insight into the trivial or mundane things experienced in actual life. You know, like filing a tax return, or changing a tyre, or applying for a bond, or how to change a nappy, or how the bloody hell to put a condom on an actual, throbbing penis.)

The headmistress – who sported an imperious set of grey curls, a posture which could rival an arrow, and the type of face that hadn't seen a smile since she'd undertaken to propagandise the promiscuity out of pubertal girls – was less than impressed with the notion that sex ed could be made fun.

The extent of our sex education, outside of the comedian's profound wisdom, included the horrors of STDs and the importance of saying 'no' – because we were powerful women on the brink of dominance and control, and we would 'run the world' by successfully avoiding anything remotely pleasurable.

Like getting moist.

The school's mantra was, 'Men will pressure you into having sex, and you must say "no".' Actually it was 'Incepto ne Desistam' (because simply saying 'May I not shrink from purpose' was far too colloquial).

Do you think they ever considered that perhaps my purpose in life was to set the bar decidedly low for success, in order that those around me get a substantial ego boost?

No, it was not considered. There was no room for my modern-day Mother Teresa/Eleanor Roosevelt act.

Selfless humanitarian that I am.

The school did not see mediocrity as a virtue or an act of self-sacrifice.

I did get one thing right, though: never have I ever shrunk.

With the school's unyielding, poker-up-the-arse approach to everything, it should not come as a surprise that, although not a private facility, the school managed to produce only successful students and boasted a 100% pass rate, without a single failure in the illustrious history of the institution.

I've not stayed in comms with enough people from my year to identify whether they have enjoyed success since matriculating. Whether they continued to say 'no' to horny humans. Or whether they managed to deny themselves anything which does not aid them in pro-feminist success.

Even though I boast the investigative prowess of an FBI agent, I have found that it is impossible to gauge former classmates' success by their online stories/reels/whatever they've come up with now, documenting every single #fabulous thing they do.

These chicks are #totallyblessed and #lovingmyhubby.

Okay, bring out the proverbial soapbox.

Surely it is impossible to be that fucking blessed all the time.

Through my extensive internet research (not to be confused with social media stalking), I have managed to identify that, by now, they have all earned degrees in something truly marvellous like law, and that the bulk of them are married, have at least one child and another #bunintheoven, and spend their time jetting off on #bespoke holidays, living in huge houses in exclusive private estates, and driving new cars (bought for them by the humans who impregnated them). Despite their degrees, most seem to be housewives, living off their impregnators' money.

Please explain to me why the actual fuck these humans study, graduate top of the class, develop ulcers the size of the Kruger National Park due to the stress of it all, and then opt to be #stayathomemoms and maintain a lifestyle which consists primarily of meeting friends for coffee, arranging play dates – often with the gym instructor rather than the kids – and asking their significant other (SO) for an increase in their allowance, so they can shop online and then hide the shit from their SO, who knows of their nonsense but ignores it because they are #workinglate?

And how exactly are they still so fucking thin? We know they aren't actually doing any gym.

Probably doing Jim, though.

#notangryjustdisappointed

But, back to the point.

While my head had been present during the Life Orientation lesson, my body had been absent on the day they taught us to say 'no'.

Moreover, since I was neither bird nor bee, human-on-human interaction really ought to have been fine.

Or so I thought.
