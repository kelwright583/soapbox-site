# DTF

I downloaded Tinder and set up my profile:

Photo – check (double chin edited out)

Bio – check (red wine, mountain air, searching for authenticity, blah blah blah...)

Settings – check (age range, sexual preference, geographical radius)

And then I began to swipe.

Boy, I had no idea that Tinder was such a meat market!

Swipe right for fillet; swipe left for off-cuts.

That simple.

Only, most of the options were less Woolies' butchery and more Shoprite clearance aisle – limp salami, mouldy chipolatas, and expired braai packs that came with all kinds of baloney. Don't even hope for a nice, thick piece of boerewors.

It made me think of that rule about not going shopping on an empty stomach: if you started swiping while you were desperate, you'd end up picking up a lot more than you actually wanted or needed.

I learnt a lot through my Tinderventures.

Talk about learning on the job.

I feel it is my duty to share my experience with other brave souls who may be looking for 'app-iness'. So, here are some simple tips that I hope will help you better navigate dating apps.

Just hang on a seccie while I get out that soapbox...

Okay.

*clears throat*

• It's crucial that you understand the lingo, including the acronyms. HEA stands for 'happily ever after'. Few men on Tinder are actually looking for this. (Even if their profiles say that they are.)

• DTF means 'down to fuck?' It is a question. The person (probably in possession of a chipolata) wants a shag and is asking you if you would like a shag too. If you had any dreams of HEA, you will be sorely disappointed. If you had any desire for some nice, thick salami, you will be equally disappointed.

• Take into account that the pictures people upload to their dating app profiles are the best they have. If the picture is shit on Tinder, the real thing is even more shit. Guaranteed.

• Some people, while they might not be rapists or murderers, are 100% stalkers. So, you must make sure your Facebook, Instagram, and any other social media profiles are on fleek (this is socials-speak for extremely good or stylish). Stalkers are excellent at researching your credentials, likes, dislikes, travel preferences, and entire family tree before they even ask if you are DTF.

• And while you're at it, get Aunty Mabel to untag that pic of you inhaling spareribs at Spur when you were 15. Nobody swipes right on a sauce-beard. And as for meat sweats – ew! Maybe just put 'vegan' in your bio, or say you enjoy #organic food. You can enjoy normal food, but not, like, enough to actually eat it. Just enjoy #foodporn. But don't use the word porn, because then people will think you are DTF.

• Even if you are not DTF, you must, at all times, portray yourself as though you are fuckable. On every socials platform.

• Image is everything. Have #cuteoutfits. Be #charming and #beautiful and make sure that your #nofilter pics have at least four filters, but, like, natural-looking ones.

• If you want lots of matches (the kind to ignite your love life, not your braaiplek), then have pictures of #sunsets and #puppies and things that seem wholesome and outdoorsy. #beachvibes will also work.

• Don't ever not be fabulous on dating apps. You must, at all times, be fabulous. As in, you want to give off wife vibes, but don't look like the kind of wife that is going to have a headache every night. Look like the wife who will be servant-hearted or good at cleaning and cooking and stuff but, like, in sexy maid outfits.

• Don't expect to meet someone who is husband material – that puts way too much pressure on the situation. (But make sure you look like wife material, because, you know, double standards.)

• Just don't expect anything. No guy likes an expector.

• Dress well, but not so well that you look unapproachable (or like you earn more than he does).

• Say cool things, like: 'eyebrows on fleek' and 'so woke'.

• Ask lots of questions so that you seem very interested in them, but don't ask too many questions in case they think you are overly keen. Also don't ask personal questions. What are you, a detective? Ask them what their third favourite dinosaur is, or their favourite takeaway meal.

• On the subject of takeaways, your favourite fast food must be something cute, like chicken nuggets. But no chips. You don't like carbs. Also, you only eat takeaways when you are super tipsy (don't ever say drunk – they will think you are DTF).

• Love CrossFit – or whatever fitness fad is en vogue. I hope for your sake it's not CrossFit, that insane tribal theatre of twisting torsos and torn ligaments, performed for pics to post on socials, and fuelled by protein powder and peer pressure. But be all in for movement and muscle. Accept that every second profile pic is like a peek at a chimp enclosure: triceps like overfilled water balloons, bulging veins, body hair, and those overbuilt shoulders closing in on their necks. And that permanent, smug grin – yuck. But guys love this, so you must too.

• Beware of new age freaks. Like people whose profiles include the words 'spiritually enlightened', 'chakra', or 'tantric'. This is the sort of guy who once spent a weekend in Salt Rock with a sangoma and now refers to himself as a 'conduit'. He's read half a book on some woowoo and wants you to reparent his inner child (preferably while naked).

• Be on form, but don't look staged. Look like you might want the occasional shag, but don't be DTF.

• Don't be vocal about wanting HEA – your swipe-rights will run a mile. Don't be vocal about any of your desires. Unless you are DTF.

• Basically, you must pretend about everything, but also be honest and upfront. But don't tell them during the first chat or text that you want eleventy squeakers. Wait until the first date.

• Don't tell a guy off the bat that you want exclusivity; you will scare him off. He may want to play the field a bit. You must be open-minded about this, but also make sure that you sleep only with him. He will be very accommodating and allow this.

• If your dating app interactions result in a date, know that the likelihood of you paying at least half of the bill is very high. This is not a bad thing. If you let these fuckers pay, they will think that you are DTF. Or a gold-digger.

• Don't be a gold-digger. Guys on dating apps don't have lots of money. You will dig and dig and come up empty handed (and with crap under your nails).

• Don't drop your standards too low, though; money is still important. You still need to eat. But don't eat too much. Only chicken nuggets. Men like women who look a bit starved. But not, like, kwashiorkor starved.

• Have an opinion, because guys like strong women, but make sure your opinion is the same as theirs.

• Let him open the car door for you and offer you his jacket when it's chilly; this affirms his masculinity. But don't be needy – it's unbecoming.

• Be becoming. Be virtuous. Be a lady. But also, be a freak in the sheets. Although don't be DTF. No one likes 'em loose.

You see, dating apps are very easy if you take heed of these few simple tips.

If there had been more men on Tinder who weren't emotionally stunted, financially feral, or shaped like a lumbering turd, perhaps I'd have found a match quickly. But the profiles were ridiculous: obvious smokescreens, shallow façades, and bios curated to look deep without actually saying anything.

I laughed.

I swiped.

I even flirted.

But somewhere between the dog pics and the gym selfies, I started to feel it: I didn't really want to be DTF.

Peckish though I was, it wasn't just about the boerewors. I was looking for a man of moral substance and complex ingredients; someone seasoned and spicy (but, let's be honest, a bit of length never ruined a wors roll).

On balance, the size of the boerewors became less important to me than quality of the filling.

I wasn't asking for much: only someone who saw me as a person – not a mirror, a muse, or an emotional mule.

I just wanted to be seen. Not scanned, not sized up, not spiritually diagnosed. Seen.

And maybe held.

Preferably not by a chakra-charming charlatan who did CrossFit.

DTF? More like WTF.
