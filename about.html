Who she actually is

Not the polished version.

At 30(ish), I suddenly find I have a lot to say about life. Not only about my own life, mind you, but other peoples' too. I have opinions on topics as varied as sex, parenting, home décor, and the unforgivable presence of fruit in a savoury salad.

I am not yet forty, not yet rich, not yet thin, and not yet in possession of a single thriving herb, and yet I consider these stellar lack of achievements the perfect qualifications to have strong views about everything and the good sense to know that social media is not the place for them - though I cannot always say the same for text messages (which have historically functioned as delivery vehicles for very long, very detailed essays that nobody asked for).

The book covers the years between 16 and 30. The ones in which I became a mother, lost myself in a couple of relationships that were, in retrospect, more situationship than relationship, and considerably more chaos than either. I gained 20 kilograms and stopped apologising for it (because what is a girl to do when she loves food and disdains exercise), and slowly - very slowly - started to figure out what I actually thought about things. (Let's be honest: I'm still not entirely sure, and this may change with the same frequency as my opinions on whether wine counts as a food group. It does.)

Moving on.

In the book I showcased a spectacular and recurring inability to run literally. Instead I did so figuratively - out of fucks, patience, money, time, and all remaining sensibilities. I now actually run (although, I use the term loosely). "Running" is generous... I shuffle. A determined, committed, not-entirely-dignified shuffle, like a waddle of wombats wobbling toward a warren, or a parade of plodding penguins who'd been told there was pilchards at the podium. (Unlike my running, the alliteration, at least, has always been effortless.) Incidentally, the chapters in the book where I say I would never run, were written before I took up this extreme sport. I choose to see this as character development. If you have no idea what I am talking about, it's because you haven't bought the book. You should.)

I write because some experiences don't improve with silence. I write because when left unsaid, things get heavier over time. I write because if I do not, some poor unsuspecting victim will likely be the receiver of an unsolicited state of the nation address, in the form of a WhatsApp or an exceedingly well worded, although decidedly harsh, email. I write because, if I've learned anything, it's that the story you're most embarrassed to tell is usually the one someone else desperately needed to hear.

The short version

How we got here.

2008 Daughter born. Matric written, under circumstances that could generously be described as suboptimal. Passed with distinction. Got all As, since you're asking, and nobody was. We don't talk about Afrikaans.
2009-2011 Single. Fucking it up enthusiastically. Running - figuratively, obviously, never literally - from responsibility, from reality, and from anything that looked like it might require sustained effort. Somehow also studying. Got a BA in Marketing, which is the thing you study when you have absolutely no idea what to study, and which conveniently positions you to one day do an MBA, which is the thing that every single person who has ever wanted to seem successful eventually does.
2012-2017 Still single. Landed a very prestigious, ultra-important, super-adventurous, not-boring-at-all position at an insurance firm. Got stable. Met Tinder Guy. Became considerably less stable.
2018 More Tinder Guy. Lost myself entirely, which was particularly frustrating given that I'd only just found myself. Navigated what I now correctly identify as a narcissist - because by 2018 narcissist content was trending and I finally had the vocabulary. Moved out. Got into significant debt acquiring very impressive crockery. Worth it.
2019 Moved back home. Cap in hand. Started writing.
2020 Met a Capetonian. Immediately decided I would marry him and have his children. Turned 30 in what can only be described as the shittest of shit years, on the shittest of shit birthdays, completely alone, in a pandemic. Did not marry the Capetonian.
2021 Did not have his children either. Or anyone else's.
2022-2024 Wrote and finished the manuscript. Discovered that telling the stories that needed breathing room - and physically cutting the ones that didn't - was its own kind of healing. Closed some chapters for good. Incidentally, and this did not make it into the book: dated someone fifteen years my senior, despite having very strong opinions about exactly this in the manuscript. Confirmed that maturity has nothing to do with age, and that large age gaps are, in fact, as annoying as I originally suspected.
2025 Met Karl. Stable, sturdy, beautifully dorky, wise, kind. Feels like home. Did not see this coming. Delighted to report it anyway.
2026 The book. Finally.
Ground rules

A few things before you dive in.

  • This isn't a guide, a manifesto, or a place to get your life sorted.
  • It's a record - written with candour, reflection, and enough humour to survive reliving my own questionable decisions.
  • Profanity appears without warning. So do opinions. Both are intentional.
  • Kindness is welcome. Curiosity too. Cruelty is not interesting.
  • Take what resonates. Leave the rest exactly where you found it.
