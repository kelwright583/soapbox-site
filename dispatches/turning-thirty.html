8 min read Growing Up Motherhood

Turning Thirty and Other Acts of Defiance

I turned thirty in a pandemic, alone, with a toddler who'd just discovered the word 'no' and a cat who'd discovered the joy of knocking things off shelves. It was less a celebration and more a survival milestone.

There was no party. No gathering of friends. No cake that said "30 and Thriving" because I was neither thirty nor thriving—I was twenty-nine and three-quarters, and I was surviving. Barely.

Instead, I spent my thirtieth birthday explaining to a two-year-old why we couldn't go to the park. Again. And why the cat couldn't have cake. And why, no, we couldn't have cake for every meal, even though it was a special day.

It wasn't what I'd imagined. When I was twenty-five, I'd pictured thirty as this magical threshold where everything would click into place. I'd have my life figured out. I'd be confident. I'd know what I was doing.

Turns out, thirty is just twenty-nine with more back pain and a better understanding of your own limitations.

Thirty taught me that milestones aren't always celebrations. Sometimes they're just markers that you've made it this far.

But here's the thing: I made it. I survived turning thirty in a global crisis, parenting solo, and learning that sometimes the best you can do is just keep showing up. Even when showing up means explaining to a toddler that no, we can't have ice cream for breakfast, even though it's Mommy's special day.

Thirty taught me that milestones aren't always celebrations. Sometimes they're just markers that you've made it this far. And sometimes, that's enough. More than enough, actually.

So here's to thirty. To surviving. To showing up. And to understanding that sometimes the best birthday gift is just making it through another day without losing your mind.
