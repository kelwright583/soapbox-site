On Woolworths Bras and Seatbelts Disguised as Straps

Thirty is the year your lingerie stops being flirtation and starts being infrastructure. The day I realized my bra was essentially a seatbelt for my tits was the day I understood I'd crossed a threshold.

I was standing in Woolworths, holding up a beige contraption that looked like it belonged in a hardware store, not a lingerie department. The sales assistant—bless her—was explaining the "support system" with the kind of enthusiasm usually reserved for explaining how a car's suspension works.

"This one has reinforced straps," she said, pointing to what appeared to be industrial-grade elastic. "And extra-wide bands for comfort."

Comfort. Right. Because at thirty, comfort has become the new sexy. I don't need lace that looks like it might break if I breathe too hard. I need something that will hold everything in place when I'm running after a toddler who's discovered the joy of sprinting toward traffic.

I bought three. Beige, black, and another beige because I'm nothing if not predictable. And as I walked out of that store, I had the distinct feeling that I'd just purchased safety equipment, not lingerie.

There's something deeply unsexy about buying bras that prioritize function over form. But there's also something liberating about it. I'm done pretending that my underwear needs to be a performance. It's infrastructure now. It's the foundation that holds everything else together.

And honestly? That's fine. Because at thirty, I've got bigger things to worry about than whether my bra straps are visible through my shirt. Like whether I remembered to pack snacks. Or whether I'm doing this whole parenting thing right. Or whether I'll ever figure out how to fold a fitted sheet.

The bras are comfortable. They do their job. And that's enough. Sometimes infrastructure is exactly what you need.
